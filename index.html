Introduction

As the largest ecosystem in the world, the Marine ecosystem is rich in water resources, biological resources, mineral resources, etc., which is vital to maintain the normal operation of the earth and the activities of human beings.

Since the construction of global industrialization, marine ecosystem has been affected at a rapid speed. With the dramatic development of economy, agriculture, industrialization and urbanization, marine environment pollution problem becomes increasingly serious, which has attracted widespread attention. Estuarine and coastal zones are vulnerable to the development of coastal cities and human activities because they are located in an important transition zone between the mainland and the ocean.

Research Interests:

Biogeochemistry and carbon cycling along the river-estuary-coastal continuum during the Anthropocene; Stable isotope geochemistry; Cycling of organic matter investigated by the molecular biomarker fingerprint and the state-of-the-art ultra-high resolution mass spectrometry; Big-data techniques (e.g., machine learning) and their application in marine organic geochemistry.
